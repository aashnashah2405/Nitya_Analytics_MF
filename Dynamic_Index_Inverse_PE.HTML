Inverse PE Cap Allocation

Live Backtest Jan 2018 → Mar 2026
What is this strategy?

This is a valuation-driven cap rotation strategy. Every month it looks at the median PE ratio of three market cap segments — large (NIFTY100, rank 1–100), mid (NIFTYMIDCAP150, rank 101–250) and small (NIFTYSMALLCAP250, rank 251–500) — and allocates capital proportionally to their inverse PE. The lower the PE of a segment, the higher the allocation to it. The idea: systematically tilt toward whichever part of the market is relatively cheaper, and away from whatever is expensive.

How are weights calculated?

For each month, compute 1/PE for each segment (the earnings yield). Normalise these three values so they sum to 100% — that becomes the target allocation. For example, if large PE = 30, mid PE = 40, small PE = 25: inverse PEs are 3.33%, 2.50%, 4.00% → normalised weights ≈ 34% large, 25% mid, 41% small. Small cap gets the most because it has the lowest PE (highest earnings yield).

Rebalancing & execution

On the 1st of every month, the PE data is published. The portfolio immediately rebalances to the new target weights, executed at the closing price of the first trading day of that month. Legs that are overweight are sold; proceeds (after tax) are used to buy underweight legs. Each month's PE snapshot thus directly drives that month's allocation.

Tax treatment (FIFO)

When selling during a rebalance, gains are taxed using the FIFO method — oldest units sold first. If a lot was held for less than 12 months, gains are taxed at STCG 20%. If held 12 months or more, at LTCG 12.5%. Losses attract no tax. There is no exit tax on the final liquidation at March 2026 — results show gross pre-liquidation value.

Portfolio construction

A fresh ₹100 portfolio is started on the first trading day of every month from January 2018 to March 2025 — 87 portfolios in total. Each is held independently and all exit on the first trading day close of March 2026. The minimum holding for any portfolio is exactly 1 year (the March 2025 cohort). Returns at 1y, 2y … 8y snapshots are measured at the first trading day close of the respective anniversary month.

Benchmark

Each portfolio is benchmarked against a simple NIFTY500 buy-and-hold — ₹100 invested in NIFTY500 at the same entry date, held untouched to the same exit. The NIFTY500 covers the full market (large + mid + small) and represents a passive alternative that requires no monthly rebalancing or tax events. Outperformance = portfolio CAGR minus NIFTY500 CAGR over the same holding period.
