The Problem

Why retail portfolios consistently underperform

Most mutual fund portfolios fail not because good funds don't exist, but because of structural and behavioural failures in how they are built and managed.

📈
Recency Bias
Investors chase recent winners, buying near peaks and exiting during drawdowns — systematically locking in losses and missing recoveries.
🔄
Portfolio Overlap
Holding multiple funds with similar underlying positions creates a high-cost, index-like portfolio that dilutes any meaningful alpha.
📉
Ignoring Downside Risk
Conventional fund selection focuses on returns alone, overlooking other parameters — allowing funds with deep drawdowns and slow recoveries to pass undetected.
🔒
No Exit Discipline
Without objective review criteria, underperforming funds are rarely removed. Poor performers persist and drag down overall returns indefinitely.
The Approach

A fully systematic, rule-based framework

Every selection, rebalancing, and exit decision follows pre-defined, objective rules — no discretionary overrides, no emotion, no market timing.

1
Universe Definition
The investable universe is restricted to equity-oriented, direct, growth, active mutual fund schemes with a minimum 5-year operating vintage. Categories include Large Cap, Large & Mid Cap, Mid Cap, Small Cap, and Flexi Cap.
Direct Plans Only Growth Option Active Funds 5Y Min Vintage 5 Categories
2
Composite Scoring
Funds are ranked within their category across non-overlapping return cohorts over 5 years (3M, 6M, 1Y windows). Recent performance (0–24 months) is weighted 2× over older cohorts, creating a composite score where lower = stronger.
Non-overlapping cohorts Category-relative ranking Recency weighted 2× Lower score = better
3
Three-Stage Filtering
After shortlisting the top 30 by composite score: (a) Index filter — fund must beat NIFTY 50 in at least one of the two most recent quarters; (b) Drawdown filter — funds with extreme underperformance during NIFTY 500 drawdown episodes are eliminated using a ±2.5 SD threshold.
Top 30 by Score NIFTY 50 Relative Filter Drawdown Elimination
4
Portfolio Construction
The top 10 qualifying funds are selected with equal allocation. If fewer than 10 qualify, unallocated capital is held in cash (assumed 5% p.a., reflecting a liquid fund post-tax return) until suitable replacements emerge.
Top 10 Funds Equal Weighting Cash at 5% p.a.
Composite Scoring Formula
Score = [ 2×(R₆ₘ,0–6 + R₆ₘ,6–12 + R₁ᵧ,12–24) + 1×(R₁ᵧ,24–36 + R₁ᵧ,36–48 + R₁ᵧ,48–60) ] ÷ N where R = Category rank for that cohort (rank 1 = best performer) N = Number of valid cohorts available Lower composite score → stronger, more consistent fund
Rank-based aggregation is preferred over raw returns to reduce sensitivity to regime-specific return magnitudes and outliers.
Rebalancing Rules

Exit with discipline, not emotion

The portfolio is reviewed monthly. Exit signals are triggered by persistent underperformance — not single-period weakness — to avoid unnecessary churn.

🔍
Underperformance Signal Generation
Each holding is measured against NIFTY 50 across the four most recent 3-month cohorts. A signal is generated when a fund underperforms in at least 2 of those 4 cohorts. A single weak quarter is never enough to trigger an exit.
⏱️
3-Month Persistence Requirement
An exit occurs only if the underperformance signal persists for 3 consecutive monthly reviews. If the fund's relative performance improves at any point, the counter resets to zero. This removes chronic underperformers while ignoring temporary weakness.
💰
Exit Costs & Refill Logic
Exited funds are sold at prevailing NAV. Proceeds are redeployed into the next qualifying fund per current selection criteria. If no suitable replacement exists, capital is held in cash at 5% p.a. until a qualifying fund emerges.
